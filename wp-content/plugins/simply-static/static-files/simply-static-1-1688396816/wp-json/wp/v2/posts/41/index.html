Social networking site Twitter has set a temporary limit on the number of tweets users can read in a day. Elon Musk, the owner of this social media, announced this last Sunday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Elon Musk announced on Twitter that a maximum of 600 posts per day can be read from accounts that are not verified. 300 posts can be read from new accounts that are not verified.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But in case of verified account, the number of daily post reading will be 6 thousand. Elon Musk says data theft has ‘reached extremes’. To combat this, user posts are temporarily limited.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Last Friday, many users were asked to log in to view tweets. Musk said the conditions were imposed on users as an immediate emergency measure.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Arguing in favor of limiting users’ tweets, Elon Musk said that Twitter’s “stacks” of data are reducing the quality of service for ordinary users.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Elon Musk bought Twitter for 4 thousand 4 billion US dollars last year after many struggles. After becoming the owner, he announced layoffs. In one blow, the employees of the company were reduced from 8 thousand to 1500.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
