British billionaire Richard Branson and Swedish aviator Per Lindstrand set a unique record. On this day in 1987, they crossed the Atlantic Ocean in a hot-air balloon. No one before them could achieve this feat. Through this, Richard and Lindstrand set a world record. Departing from Maine, USA, they landed in Limavady, Northern Ireland. Passed 4 thousand 947 kilometers or about 3 thousand 74 miles.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

First automobile on the way<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

On July 3, 1886, the world’s first automobile was driven on German roads. The three-wheeled motor wagon was driven by engineer Karl Benz.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Longest ring chain<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

3 July 2004. In Cleveland, USA, a group of magicians made a chain by connecting steel rings one by one. The leader of this group was magician Monty Witt. There were 129 other magicians in the team. The chain they made by connecting 534 steel rings in a row set a world record.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
